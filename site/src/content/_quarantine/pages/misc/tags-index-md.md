\# Tags & Concepts

This archive uses a controlled tag system to connect documents across time, topic, and place.

Tags are not keywords.

They are \*\*analytical tools\*\*.

They allow readers to:

\- trace patterns across eras,

\- identify recurring power mechanisms,

\- and understand how different decisions connect.

---

\## Tag Categories

\### Power Channels

Broad categories describing \*how\* power operates.

\- Rules

\- Gatekeepers

\- Money

\- Courts

\- Geography

\- Narrative

---

\### Power Mechanisms

Specific methods through which power is exercised or preserved.

Examples include:

\- procedural supremacy

\- preemption

\- disenfranchisement

\- judicial delay

---

\### Policy Areas

Domains of public policy affected by governance decisions.

Examples include:

\- elections & representation

\- education

\- labor & economy

\- criminal justice

---

\### Places

Geographic communities impacted by decisions.

Examples include:

\- counties

\- cities

\- regions

---
