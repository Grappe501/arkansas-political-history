\# Judicial Delay

\## Definition

Judicial delay occurs when prolonged legal processes functionally prevent or postpone change.

---

\## Why It Matters

Delay can:

\- exhaust resources,

\- maintain status quo outcomes,

\- and avoid decisive rulings.

Justice delayed can be justice denied.

---

\## Arkansas Context

Judicial delay has been used to:

\- stall ballot initiatives,

\- postpone enforcement of rights,

\- defer accountability.

Delay often favors existing power structures.
