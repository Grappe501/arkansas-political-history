\# Disenfranchisement

\## Definition

Disenfranchisement refers to the removal or dilution of political participation through legal, procedural, or structural means.

---

\## Why It Matters

Disenfranchisement allows power to persist without majority consent.

It may be:

\- explicit (voting bans),

\- procedural (access barriers),

\- or structural (district design).

---

\## Arkansas Context

Disenfranchisement has occurred through:

\- racial exclusion under Jim Crow,

\- at-large voting systems,

\- runoff elections with suppressed turnout,

\- modern procedural barriers.
