\# Power Mechanisms

Power mechanisms describe the \*specific ways\* authority is exercised, protected, or insulated.

They recur across eras, institutions, and policy areas.

Understanding these mechanisms makes patterns visible.

---

\## Core Power Mechanisms

\- \*\*Procedural Supremacy\*\*

\- \*\*Preemption\*\*

\- \*\*Disenfranchisement\*\*

\- \*\*Executive Weakening\*\*

\- \*\*Judicial Delay\*\*

Additional mechanisms will be added as the archive grows.

---

\## How to Read These Pages

Each mechanism page includes:

\- a definition,

\- why it matters,

\- Arkansas-specific examples,

\- and links to related pages.

Mechanisms are analytical tools, not accusations.
